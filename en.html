Some data on the region of Central Asia

The Central Asia is a region in Asia, and comprises: Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan.
Located in the center of the Eurasian continent, Central Asia has a surface of ​​3,882,000 sq. km, and a population of about 70 million.

Natural resources and general economic data:

Kazakhstan
oil, gas, coal, iron ore, manganese, chromium, nickel, cobalt, copper, molybdenum, lead, zinc, bauxites, gold, uranium.

Uzbekistan
natural gas, oil, coal, gold, uranium, silver, copper, lead, zinc, tungsten, molybdenum.

Kyrgyzstan
hydropower resources; gold, and rare earth metals; coal, oil, gas; nepheline, mercury, bismuth, lead, zinc.

Tajikistan
hydropower resources, uranium, mercury, coal, lead, zinc, antimony, tungsten.

Turkmenistan
oil, natural gas, coal, sulfur, salt.

As can be seen from the above data, the region is very rich in natural resources and is attractive for investors.

Tourism

The Central Asian countries have great potential for the development of tourism.
Medieval monuments of Samarkand, Bukhara, Khiva, Shakhrisabz, Merv, Penjikent are renowned worldwide. Such "pearls" of ancient architecture as ensembles of Shahi Zinda, Bibi-Khanum, Registan Square, the mausoleum of the Samanids, the tomb of the Gur-Emir, Kalan Minaret, Ichan Kala museum are magnificent in its grand architectural design and splendor of their decorative art.
They are comparable with the architectural masterpieces of Egypt, India, China, ancient Greece and Rome.

Kazakhstan
Guided tours across the country, ethnic and eco tours, health tourism, hunting, fishing, mountain climbing

Uzbekistan
Visiting of medieval architecture masterpieces of Samarkand, Bukhara, Khiva, sports tourism, gastronomic tourism, eco tourism

Kyrgyzstan
Excursions, sports tourism, mountain climbing, mountain skiing

Tajikistan
Educational sightseeing tours to the historical Buddhist, Zoroastrian, Christian and Islamic monuments, sports tourism, gastronomic tourism

Tukmenistan
Guided tours of the sites of ancient towns, the Turkmen carpets manufacture process, the Akhal-Teke stud farms.

The region’s tourism industry is very attractive for investment due to its diversity, contrasts and colorfulness.

Since nearly all of the great religions such as Zoroastrism, Buddhism, Christianity and Islam were present in this region, all of them have left a mark in the culture of the indigenous peoples which are attractive for tourism development.
